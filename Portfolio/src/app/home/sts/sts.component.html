Being an Intern at the Special Telecommunications Service

Between July 14th and August 20th, I completed an internship at the Special Telecommunications Service. I had been accepted for this position a few months prior, after successfully passing a rigorous recruitment process. Like other students who were not originally from Bucharest, I was assigned to complete the internship in my home county. My work covered a wide range of areas, from configuring network equipment such as Cisco and Juniper switches to conducting on-site testing and collaborating closely with my more experienced colleagues. I also gained foundational theoretical knowledge in telecommunications, learning about different types of antennas and transmission media, understanding the components of a power station, and visiting an antenna site to see real-world applications and explanations.
